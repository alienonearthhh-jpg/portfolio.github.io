The Problem + Research Foundation
The barrier
ENTRY, NOT CAPABILITY
Introverted individuals are not less socially capable — initiating interaction simply requires more effort and carries a stronger expectation of negative outcomes (Wilt & Revelle, 2009). The problem is starting, not sustaining.
Research finding — Li et al. 2025
SIGNALS = CONNECTIONS
Explicit signals of openness significantly increase the likelihood of strangers forming new connections by reducing uncertainty around whether interaction is welcome. Pip is that signal.
Concept — Tamir et al. 2024
33% IS PLENTY
"Even 10% is fine — I'd want to figure out what that 10% is. It feels a bit magical." (H1) — Stranger conversations become more enjoyable as people gradually establish common ground. Pip starts that process.
Our answer
WEAR IT
TAP IT
MEET PEOPLE
Badge-worn, NFC-tap to connect, compatibility score + conversation prompt. No awkward opener required.

About Us
Sweet 16 · UTS · 42070 · Autumn 2026

Pip was developed over five sprints as part of 42070 Prototyping Physical Interaction at UTS. Through user research, paper prototyping, hardware development, and usability testing with real participants, the project evolved into a wearable device designed to support social connection.

Combining research, interaction design, engineering, and visual identity, Pip explores how technology can act as a social catalyst — helping people take the first step, not replacing the conversation itself.
